Learning by Doing - Industrial Experience @ MIT

When the industry and society want you to be career-ready after college, you need to have experience working on real machines. MIT has the same facilities that the industries use in their laboratories for production and research. The faculty at our facilities bring decades of industry expertise to the campus. When trained in such an environment, students are ready and productive from day one -to join a firm or start their business. 

State of the art world-class industry scale training facilities at MIT: 

  • Automation & Mechatronics by Siemens Germany
  • Robotics by Kuka Germany
  • 3 D Printing by Stratasys USA
  • Injection molding by Milacron USA/India
  • Machining center by Bharat Fritz Werner Germany/India
  • Civil and structural non-destructive testing instruments from Proceq SA of Switzerland
  • NABL accredited analytical & environmental testing laboratory with instruments from Shimadzu Japan
  • Agriculture research & development center with Israeli setup
  • Center for advanced materials research with instruments from the Malvern United Kingdom
  • Nano Technology Electrospray by Spraybase Ireland
Engineering Exploration

The first-year students begin with engineering exploration, design thinking, problem-solving, teamwork, and building products.

Expert Interaction

Students interact with subject matter experts while graduating, which helps them connect theory with practice.

Team Spirit

Students are encouraged to work in teams to develop a deeper understanding of concepts and benefit from peer to peer learning.
